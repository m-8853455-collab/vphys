Work–Energy Theorem

VPhys – The Virtual Physics Lab

Statement

The Work–Energy Theorem states that the net work done on an object is equal to the change in its kinetic energy.

Formulas

Formula Meaning
W = ΔKE Net work equals change in kinetic energy
KE = ½mv² Kinetic energy

Example

A 2 kg object accelerates from rest to 4 m/s.

Work done = ½ × 2 × (4² − 0²) = 16 J
